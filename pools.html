F.A.Q.

How is an FSP Pool different from GoFundMe or Kickstarter?
Those are donation platforms; when you give money, it's gone. FSP Pools are Deposit platforms. You deposit funds into a secure, principal-protected vault. You retain ownership of your capital, while the yield generated by the pool is directed to the cause (Athlete, Tournament, or Team). It is the first funding model where you can support a passion without losing your principal.
How does the recipient get paid if I keep my money?
We utilize institutional-grade financial rails to generate Infrastructure Yield on the pooled assets. By aggregating thousands of deposits, we create a massive capital base. The yield from that base—paid out daily or weekly—funds the athlete's travel, the tournament's prize money, or the team's operations.
Can I withdraw my money?
Yes. Your principal is yours. Depending on the specific pool's terms, there may be a short "vesting period" to allow yield to generate, but after that, you are free to withdraw your original deposit. You are allocating the time value of your money, not the money itself.
Why should I "stake" an athlete instead of just sponsoring them?
Sponsoring is an expense; Staking is an asset. When you stake an athlete via AthleteStash, you become part of their journey. You get access to exclusive updates, "Inner Circle" perks, and FSP Champion Points, all while keeping your money safe. It turns fandom into a partnership.
How can a sponsorship actually be "Zero Cost"?
It changes the mechanics from spending to allocating. Instead of paying a $100k fee to a team (which is a sunk cost), a brand deposits a larger sum of capital into a Sponsor Pool. The yield from that capital pays the team their fee over time. Instead of parking funds in a corporate account, a brand can utilise those funds for additional visibility at no sunk cost. At the end of the contract, the brand withdraws their original principal. The cost to the brand is the opportunity cost of the capital, not the capital itself.
What is a "Purse Pool"?
It's a way for fans to force the world to pay attention to their sport. By pooling capital into a tournament's prize vault, the community swells the jackpot. This attracts better TV coverage and higher-ranked pros. You aren't paying the winner; the yield on your deposit is. You are essentially "crowdsourcing prestige" for your favorite sport for stakeholders to realize the value in your sport.
Does the brand get the same visibility?
Yes. The team receives their funding and the brand receives their assets (jersey patch, billboards, digital rights) exactly as they would in a traditional deal. The only difference is the financial plumbing behind the payment.
Is "Fan Participate" gambling?
No. You are not betting on an outcome to win money. You are pooling resources to buy an action. For example, a pool of fans might contribute to "Unlock a Power-Up" for their team or "Trigger a Replay Review." It is a collective purchase of influence, not a wager.
What do I earn for participating?
Active participants earn FSP Champion Points and Golden Tickets. These can be redeemed for rewards, exclusive merchandise, or entries into our "Money Can't Buy" experience drawings (like trips to the World Cup or F1). The more you engage, the more status you earn in the ecosystem.
